Privacy Policy

Quraan Arabi Academy ("the Academy," "we," "us") is committed to protecting your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains what data we collect, how we use it, how we protect it, and what your rights are. By enrolling in any programme or using our website, you agree to the practices described here.

1. What Information We Collect

When you submit a Placement Evaluation, apply for a programme, or make a purchase, we collect:

  • Identity data: Full name, age, and profile photo (optional)
  • Contact data: Email address, phone number, and country of residence
  • Academic data: Qur'an memorisation level, recitation audio uploads, study goals, and progress notes
  • Transaction data: Payment records, plan selection, and billing cycle
  • Technical data: IP address, browser type, and general device information when you visit our website
Recitation Audios: Audio files you upload for placement evaluation are used exclusively by our instructors for academic assessment purposes. They are stored securely and are never shared with third parties for commercial use.
2. How We Use Your Information

We use your personal data for the following purposes:

  • Processing your enrolment application and placement evaluation
  • Scheduling and delivering live sessions via Zoom
  • Communicating academic updates, session reminders, and progress reports
  • Sending marketing emails about new programmes, resources, and offers — with your consent
  • Managing your student account and subscription billing
  • Improving our programmes and website experience
  • Complying with legal obligations where applicable

We will never sell your personal data to third parties. We do not engage in profiling for advertising purposes.

3. Data Storage & Security

Your data is stored securely using Firebase, part of Google Cloud infrastructure, which employs industry-standard encryption in transit (TLS) and at rest. Access to student data is strictly limited to authorised Academy staff and instructors.

We take reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect your information from unauthorised access, loss, or misuse. No system is completely immune to risk; however, we are committed to responding promptly to any suspected breach.

Important: Please keep your account credentials confidential. The Academy is not liable for unauthorised access resulting from your failure to protect your login details.
4. Cookies & Tracking

Our website uses minimal cookies to support core functionality — such as maintaining your session state and remembering language preferences. We do not use invasive advertising trackers or third-party marketing cookies.

Standard analytics (such as Google Analytics) may be used in anonymised form to understand how visitors use our site. No personally identifiable information is shared with analytics providers.

You may disable non-essential cookies through your browser settings without affecting access to our core services.

6. International Students

Our Academy serves students worldwide. If you are located in the European Union or United Kingdom, you have rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) including the right to access, correct, and request deletion of your data. If you are in California, USA, similar rights apply under the CCPA.

By submitting your application from any country, you consent to your data being processed as described in this policy, including storage on Google Cloud servers which may be located outside your home country. Such transfers are conducted under appropriate safeguards.

7. Children's Data (Under 18)

If a student is under the age of 18, enrolment requires written consent from a parent or legal guardian. The parent or guardian will serve as the primary account holder and must agree to this Privacy Policy on the student's behalf.

We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 13 without verified parental consent. If we become aware that data has been collected from a child under 13 without such consent, we will delete it promptly.

Parents may request to view, correct, or delete their child's data at any time by contacting us directly.
8. Your Data Rights & Deletion Requests

You have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you
  • Correct inaccurate information in your student profile
  • Delete your account and associated data
  • Withdraw consent to marketing communications at any time
  • Export your data in a portable format upon request

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at the email below. We will respond within 14 business days. Note that deleting your account will permanently remove your academic records, progress reports, and certificates.

Terms of Service

These Terms of Service govern your enrolment in and use of Quraan Arabi Academy's programmes, website, and digital products. By applying or enrolling, you confirm that you have read, understood, and agree to be bound by these terms. Please read them carefully.

1. The Academy & Its Services

Quraan Arabi Academy is an international online educational institution offering:

  • Structured Qur'an and Arabic programmes delivered via live Zoom sessions
  • Premium Elite Mentorship programmes
  • The Founder's Ijazah Track — a selective, application-based scholarly mentorship
  • Digital educational products including PDF workbooks and study kits

All programmes are delivered online. Students are responsible for ensuring a stable internet connection and access to the Zoom platform. The Academy is not liable for technical issues on the student's end that result in missed or disrupted sessions.

2. Enrolment & Eligibility

To enrol in any programme, you must:

  • Complete the Placement Evaluation form accurately and truthfully
  • Be at least 5 years of age (students under 18 require parental consent)
  • Have access to a device with audio/video capability for live sessions
  • Agree to these Terms of Service, the Privacy Policy, and the Refund Policy

For the Elite Mentorship and Ijazah Track programmes, admission is selective and at the Academy's sole discretion. Completing an application does not guarantee acceptance. The Academy reserves the right to decline any application without being required to provide a reason.

3. Subscriptions & Payments

Standard programmes are offered on a monthly subscription basis. Payment is due at the beginning of each billing cycle. Failure to pay may result in suspension of access pending resolution.

  • Prices are stated in USD and are subject to change with 30 days' notice to existing subscribers
  • Trial sessions, where offered, are governed by the terms stated at the point of booking
  • The Academy uses secure third-party payment processors and does not store your card details directly
  • Any applicable taxes (VAT, GST, etc.) are the student's responsibility based on their country of residence
4. Sessions, Scheduling & Attendance

Session times are agreed upon between the student and their assigned instructor. Students are expected to attend sessions punctually and prepared.

  • Rescheduling: Requests must be made at least 24 hours before the scheduled session
  • Late arrivals: Sessions will not be extended to compensate for late starts caused by the student
  • No-shows: A session missed without prior notice will be counted as used and is non-recoverable
  • Instructor absence: If your instructor is unable to conduct a session, a replacement session will be scheduled at no additional cost or a credit applied
5. Student Conduct & Academic Integrity

All students are expected to engage with the Academy in good faith and with respect for the sacred nature of the material being studied. The following conduct is strictly prohibited:

  • Misrepresenting your academic background or memorisation level during application
  • Recording sessions without the explicit written consent of your instructor and the Academy
  • Sharing, distributing, or reselling any Academy materials, session recordings, or digital products
  • Engaging in disrespectful, abusive, or inappropriate behaviour towards instructors or Academy staff
  • Using the Academy's name, brand, or materials for commercial purposes without written permission

The Academy reserves the right to terminate enrolment immediately and without refund if any of the above violations occur.

6. Intellectual Property

All materials produced by Quraan Arabi Academy — including digital workbooks, session notes, curriculum structures, video recordings, and website content — are the intellectual property of the Academy and its Founder. Unauthorised reproduction or distribution is strictly prohibited and may be subject to legal action.

Students are granted a personal, non-transferable licence to use purchased digital products for their own private study only.

7. Limitation of Liability

The Academy provides educational services in good faith and strives to deliver the highest quality instruction. However, we cannot guarantee specific academic outcomes, including but not limited to the completion of Ijazah certification, attainment of particular fluency levels, or memorisation targets, within any defined timeframe.

The Academy shall not be liable for any indirect, incidental, or consequential losses arising from enrolment, including technical disruptions, instructor changes, or scheduling adjustments made in good faith.

8. Cancellation & Termination

Students may cancel their subscription at any time by providing written notice. Cancellation takes effect at the end of the current billing cycle. Please refer to the Refund & Cancellation Policy below for detailed terms regarding refunds.

The Academy reserves the right to terminate a student's enrolment — with written notice where circumstances permit — in cases of persistent non-attendance, conduct violations, or non-payment. Where termination is initiated by the Academy without student fault, a pro-rata refund will be issued for the unused portion of the current period.

9. Governing Law

These Terms of Service shall be governed by and construed in accordance with applicable international educational standards and good-faith principles. For students in the EU/UK, applicable consumer protection laws apply. Any disputes will first be addressed through direct communication; the Academy is committed to fair and transparent resolution.

No Ijazah Guarantee: Paying the programme fee does not guarantee receipt of an Ijazah. Certification is a scholarly judgement made exclusively by the Founder based on the student's demonstrated mastery, conduct, and academic integrity.

Ijazah Track Special Agreement

This document governs the terms of the Founder's Ijazah Track — a private, selective scholarly mentorship offered by Quraan Arabi Academy under the direct supervision of Mentor Mohamed Abdul-Maqsoud. By accepting admission to this programme, the student acknowledges and agrees to every condition stated below in full.

This is a binding agreement. Acceptance of a place in the Ijazah Track constitutes your explicit acceptance of all terms in this document, in addition to the General Terms of Service and Privacy Policy. Please read carefully before proceeding.
1. Selective Admission

Admission to the Ijazah Track is not automatic and not guaranteed. Each application is reviewed personally by Mentor Mohamed Abdul-Maqsoud, who assesses the student's academic readiness, character, sincerity of intention, and suitability for a scholarly transmission programme.

  • The Academy reserves the right to decline any application at its sole discretion, without obligation to provide a reason
  • Meeting the stated prerequisites does not guarantee admission
  • Acceptance is personal and non-transferable — another individual may not take a student's place
  • Admission may be revoked prior to programme commencement if it is found that the application contained inaccurate or misleading information
2. No Guaranteed Completion Timeline

The Ijazah Track is a scholarly programme measured by mastery, not by time. There is no fixed duration for the programme. Completion depends entirely on the student's progress, consistency, and the academic judgement of the Mentor.

  • Estimates given during the application process are indicative only and do not constitute a contractual commitment
  • Students must not make personal or professional arrangements contingent on a specific completion date
  • The Mentor will provide periodic progress assessments to keep the student informed of their standing
Some students may complete the programme within months; others may require years. This is the nature of legitimate scholarly transmission — it is earned, not purchased.
3. Attendance Requirements

The Ijazah Track demands rigorous, consistent engagement. The following attendance standards are mandatory:

  • Students must attend all scheduled sessions unless prior notice of at least 48 hours is given
  • A maximum of 2 unexcused absences per month is permitted. Exceeding this threshold may result in a formal warning or programme suspension
  • Three or more consecutive unexcused absences constitute automatic programme withdrawal unless exceptional circumstances are communicated and accepted by the Mentor
  • Sessions may not be delegated or attended by another person on the student's behalf
  • Students are expected to arrive prepared — having completed any assigned revision, memorisation, or practice assigned in the previous session
4. Ethical Conduct

The Ijazah Track involves a direct scholarly relationship with an Islamic scholar of high standing. Students are expected to conduct themselves accordingly at all times — within sessions and in their broader conduct.

  • Students must engage with the programme with sincerity, humility, and genuine scholarly intent
  • Disrespectful, dismissive, or inappropriate behaviour towards the Mentor or Academy staff will result in immediate programme termination without refund
  • Students may not share private communications, session content, or feedback from the Mentor without explicit written permission
  • The student's broader public conduct, particularly in matters related to Islamic scholarship and the Academy's reputation, is considered relevant to their continued participation
The Ijazah represents a sacred scholarly chain. Any conduct that brings the programme, the Mentor, or the Academy into disrepute is grounds for immediate withdrawal of both programme access and any pending or issued certification.
5. Academic Integrity

The integrity of the Ijazah certification depends on authentic, verifiable scholarly progression. The following are strictly prohibited:

  • Misrepresenting memorisation levels or recitation quality at any stage of the programme
  • Having another individual attend or complete assessments in the student's place
  • Using AI tools, recordings of other reciters, or any other form of misrepresentation during evaluation sessions
  • Claiming an Ijazah from Quraan Arabi Academy prior to formal issuance by the Founder

Any discovered breach of academic integrity will result in immediate termination of the programme, full forfeiture of all fees paid, and if applicable, revocation of any issued certificate. The Academy may also take steps to notify relevant scholarly networks.

6. Certification & The Founder's Right of Withdrawal

The formal Ijazah, with the Sanad chain attached, is issued exclusively by Mentor Mohamed Abdul-Maqsoud upon his personal scholarly judgement that the student has achieved the required standard.

  • Certification is not guaranteed regardless of fees paid, sessions attended, or time invested
  • The Founder reserves the right to withhold or decline to issue certification at any point if, in his scholarly assessment, the required standard has not been met
  • The Founder reserves the right to revoke a previously issued Ijazah if it is subsequently discovered that the student misrepresented their abilities, violated academic integrity, or engaged in conduct that renders the transmission inappropriate
  • A revoked Ijazah will be formally communicated to the student in writing and, where appropriate, noted in the Academy's scholarly records
The Ijazah is a scholarly trust. It carries the weight of an unbroken chain connecting the holder to the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. It is issued with full seriousness and bears responsibilities. Its issuance is a scholarly decision — not a commercial transaction.
7. Programme Suspension & Termination

The Academy or Mentor may suspend or terminate a student's participation in the Ijazah Track under the following circumstances:

  • Repeated failure to meet attendance requirements
  • Failure to demonstrate meaningful academic progress over a sustained period
  • Breach of ethical conduct or academic integrity standards
  • Non-payment of programme fees
  • Any other circumstance in which the Mentor, in his scholarly judgement, determines that continuation is not appropriate

Where suspension is temporary, the student will be given a defined period to remediate the issue. Permanent termination is final and will be communicated in writing.

8. Confidentiality

The content of Ijazah Track sessions — including the Mentor's personal scholarly commentary, correction methodology, and programme materials — is strictly confidential. Students agree not to:

  • Record sessions without written consent from the Mentor
  • Share programme materials or session content with any third party
  • Post session extracts, audio, or methodology publicly without prior written approval from the Academy
9. Agreement Acknowledgement

By accepting admission to the Ijazah Track and making the first programme payment, the student confirms that they have:

  • Read and understood this Ijazah Track Special Agreement in full
  • Accepted all terms, conditions, and obligations set out herein
  • Understood and accepted the refund and cancellation terms specific to this programme
  • Consented to the Academy's data practices as described in the Privacy Policy
